Strategic Analysis · Event Format

Comparison of event formats
for C-level automotive industry events

Confidential Decision Brief
March 2026
⭐ Recommendation

Preferred approach: Physical event with limited hybrid elements — optimal for C-level engagement, trust-building, and deeper dialogue. Complement with selective digital participation only where geographic barriers justify it.

🏛️
Physical Event
All participants and speakers gather on-site. Creates the conditions for trust, candid conversations, and networking that are difficult to replicate digitally — especially at C-level.
★ Recommended
Advantages
  • Builds genuine trust and personal relationships
  • Enables spontaneous, candid side conversations
  • Higher engagement and attention during sessions
  • Strong networking in corridors and breaks
  • Clear signal of organizational commitment and priority
Challenges
  • Geographic constraint — requires travel for international participants
  • Higher costs for venue, catering, and logistics
  • Longer planning lead time required
  • Potentially lower attendance due to travel barriers
Best For
  • Strategic decision-making meetings at C-level
  • Relationship-building with key stakeholders
  • Sensitive discussions requiring trust and confidentiality
  • High-prestige industry summits
🎬
Physical + Pre-recorded International Speakers
The event runs physically, but international speakers contribute via professionally pre-recorded video. Delivers control and polish, but eliminates direct interaction with remote speakers.
Alternative
Advantages
  • Full production control — no technical failures with pre-recorded material
  • Polished, professional delivery from all speakers
  • Enables global experts to participate without travel costs
  • On-site networking and atmosphere fully preserved
Challenges
  • Zero live interaction with international speakers
  • Pre-recorded video feels passive — lowers room energy
  • Requires pre-production coordination and scheduling
  • Speakers cannot adapt message to live audience mood
Best For
  • Keynotes from globally recognised experts with high production value
  • Situations where live technology is deemed too risky
  • A complement within an otherwise dynamic physical programme
🌐
Hybrid — Physical + Live via Teams
The core event runs physically while international participants join live via Microsoft Teams. Combines global reach with in-person presence, but places high demands on technology and facilitation.
Alternative
Advantages
  • Enables live participation regardless of geography
  • On-site group retains full networking effect
  • Flexible — participants can choose their format
  • Remote participants can contribute via chat and Q&A
Challenges
  • High execution risk — live tech failures undermine prestige
  • Remote participants receive a noticeably inferior experience
  • Requires dedicated AV resources and full rehearsal
  • Facilitator must manage two audiences simultaneously
  • Risk of uneven dynamics between physical and digital groups
Best For
  • Keynotes with globally relevant speakers who cannot travel
  • Organisations with strong AV capabilities and production budget
  • Events where reach is valued as highly as depth
💻
Fully Online via Microsoft Teams
The entire event is delivered digitally via Teams. Technically fully feasible for large groups — Microsoft Teams supports up to 1,000 participants per meeting and offers interaction features including chat, Q&A, and polls.
Alternative
Advantages
  • Unlimited geographic reach — global participation with no travel
  • Lower cost per participant — no venue or catering required
  • Easy to scale: hosting 100 participants on Teams is fully feasible
  • Built-in interaction: live chat, Q&A, reactions, and polls
  • Simple recording and post-event distribution
Challenges
  • Weak for networking and trust-based dialogue
  • Lower attention and engagement — competition from email and distractions
  • Suboptimal for strategic C-level conversations
  • No opportunity for spontaneous side discussions
Best For
  • Information and update sessions with a broad audience
  • Situations with strict travel restrictions or short lead times
  • A complement to physical events (e.g. follow-up webinars)
ℹ️ Technical note: It is fully feasible to host 100 participants on Microsoft Teams. The platform supports large meetings and offers interaction features including live chat, Q&A, and reactions — more than sufficient for a well-structured digital event.
